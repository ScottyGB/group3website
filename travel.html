Travel

Manchester is easy to access and well-known for its transport links in and out of the city.

Trams

Manchesters Metrolink is a great way to get into and around Manchester. With the average trams arriving every 12 minutes. This mode of transport is one of the most reliable in Manchester. A Zone 4 ticket will get you all around the City Centre. Tickets are discounted if purchased during off-peak times.

Trains

The main Train Stations to get in and out of Manchester are Piccadilly, Oxford Road, Deansgate and Victoria. Due to strikes they are probably not the most reliable of transport routes. However when they are running they are a comfortable experience in themselves. Tickets can be bought at the rail station, on the train and online.

Buses

Greater Manchester is starting to bring back buses under local control. This is to ensure passengers are provided with a better, consistent service. Fares and tickets are price capped at £2 for a single journey and £5 for a day of unlimited travel. Tickets can be bought on board or online. Manchester also provides a free bus service around the city centre. Feeling brave? A number of night buses run in the evenings to help you get home safely.

Taxis

Nothing says city centre travel more than grabbing a taxi. There a different companies around the city or you can use good old Uber. Prices are dependent on distance and time-taken.

Car

If traveling by car, make sure you leave plenty of time as traveling around the city centre can be tricky due to roadworks and one-way streets. There are plenty of parking options, just make sure you have a ticket. Tickets can be brought via ticket machines or using an apps via your smartphone.

Bikes

Manchester has a number of cycling routes across the city. Use your own, or use Cycle Hire. Pedal bikes cost 50p to unlock and 5p per minute to ride. E-bikes cost £1 to unlock and 10p per minute to ride. You will need to be able to download the app on your smartphone to access this.

Walking

Surely one the best ways to see Manchester is on foot. It allows you to take in the friendly northern atmosphere and see the stunning architecture surrounding the city. A lot of the time it is the quickest way to get from A to B. No getting stuck in traffic or being unreliable. Did we forget to mention that it is good for you?
